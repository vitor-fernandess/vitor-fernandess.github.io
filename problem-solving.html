problem solving

When studying or working with programming you will get stuck and find a few problems very often. It is pretty common in reality, and the best thing you can do to be prepared is to have sort of a “solving problems plan” to either minimize the problem or to find a way to solve the problem. In my case for example I get stuck all the time but I always manage to solve the problem.
I was working on a project and I got stuck in a block of code that was giving me an error but I had no clue why it was giving me an error. I was feeling frustrated and sad but after 15 mins stuck in that block of code, I decided to go make myself a coffee and then try again, when I came back I realized that I was not reading the error message properly, I was only reading “Error” and trying to figure it out the rest by staring at my code, but when I read the full error message I managed to solve it. Since then I learned that reading the error message is really important and it can give a little hint of what area is not working or what we did wrong in the code.

rubber ducky method


The Rubber ducky Method is one of the best techniques to use before starting jumping straight into the problem to solve it. I feel very comfortable using the Rubber Ducky method to break the problem into small chunks like a to-do list.

Pseudocode


Pseudo code is basically writing a detailed and readable description of what the program or algorithm should do. I Feel very confident since it uses “simple”natural syntax and formatting that can be easily understood.

reading error messages


I believe that Reading error messages is pretty self explanatory. And I do feel very confident when reading it and understanding what the message says.

console.logging


Console.logging refers to “console.log()” frequently to check if the code is running, the values of a code, and if it matches what you expected it to be. I feel a little confident, and it's definitely something that I should be doing more.

googling


I would say googling is the most important problem solving technique to master. I feel super confident googling for help and also searching up stuff that I am not familiar with.

asking for help


I learned that I do not have to feel shy when asking for help. It was really hard for me to ask for help because I would feel a little stupid for not knowing a “basic” of something that we had studied before. I had to watch a few youtube videos and visit a few web pages to understand which was time consuming and make me fall back a bit. However, I understand better now that people in my cohort are more than welcome to help me with anything that I need related to the course, especially the facilitators. It took me a while to understand that, but now I feel really confident to ask for help.
